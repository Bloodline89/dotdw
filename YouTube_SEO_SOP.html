I. Pre-Production & Planning

Step Detail & Goal
1. Keyword Research Complete keyword research, identifying the primary target keyword and 3-4 supporting keywords.
2. Video Length Aim for 8-9 minutes (target 536 seconds) for optimal performance. Videos 8-12 minutes tend to rank best when retention stays high.
3. Script Generation Use a clear, detailed prompt with ChatGPT to generate the script.
4. Script Prompt Format Request the script in a table format with columns for: Timestamp, Voiceover (exact script), Visual Scene (what camera shows), Keywords to Speak, and Pattern Break Type.
5. Script Must Include The Hook, a CTA, a Session Extension, and be informed by 2026 YouTube SEO best practices.
6. Plan Chapters While scripting, define 4-6 chapter timestamps (e.g., 0:00 Intro, 1:30 First Trend). These will be added to the description after upload.
7. Plan Shorts Identify 2-3 moments in the script that work as standalone YouTube Shorts (under 60 seconds). Plan these during scripting, not as an afterthought.

II. Scripting & On-Camera Delivery

Step Detail & Goal
1. Hook (0-10s) The hook must be delivered in the first 0-10 seconds and state a specific outcome for the viewer.
2. Primary Keyword (first 10s) Say the primary keyword and any secondary high-priority keywords (e.g., "wearable trends," "2026 fashion") within the first 10 seconds so YouTube understands the topic.
3. Second Hook (20-30s) Add a "second hook" or open loop at the 20-30 second mark. This is the critical retention gate — if viewers get past 30 seconds, they're likely to stay. Example: "But first, there's one trend that completely surprised me..."
4. Natural Conversation After the hook, Hayley should talk naturally about the topic. The algorithm rewards natural, engaging content that keeps viewers watching, not forced keyword repetition.
5. Pattern Breaks Include pattern breaks every 20-30 seconds throughout the video to maintain viewer attention.
6. Clear Statements for AI When covering key points, frame them as clear statements or Q&A: "The biggest trend for 2026 is..." or "The best way to style a tulle skirt is...". AI tools parse transcripts and cite clear, quotable language.
7. CTA (60-70% mark) Include a specific, question-based CTA at the 60-70% mark of the video. Ask viewers to comment with their answer.
8. Session Extension Include a prompt at the close of the video to link to the next video in the series.
The 30-Second Rule: YouTube data shows the first 30 seconds determines whether most viewers stay or leave. Your SOP already nails the 0-10s hook — the second hook at 20-30s locks them in.

III. Post-Production & Upload

Step Detail & Goal
1. Rename Video File Rename the final video file with the primary keyword before uploading (e.g., wearable-fashion-trends-2026.mp4).
2. Title Include the primary keyword in the first 60 characters. Avoid clickbait. Front-load the keyword as early as possible in the title.
3. Custom Thumbnail Design a custom thumbnail: high-contrast, one focal point, readable text. If CTR is below 4-5% after 48 hours, swap the thumbnail immediately.
4. Description (200-300 words) Write a 200-300 word description with keywords naturally distributed. Include:
  • Primary keyword in the first 1-2 sentences
  • 2-3 target keywords + 3-5 related keywords throughout
  • Relevant external links
5. Chapters / Timestamps Add manual chapter timestamps in the description (planned during pre-production). Must start with 0:00. Chapters appear in Google search results and improve CTR.
6. Hashtags Add 3-5 hashtags in the description (e.g., #FashionTrends2026 #TulleSkirt #SustainableFashion). These create clickable topic links and aid discovery.
7. Tags Add 5-8 tags: primary keyword, 2-3 keyword variations, 1-2 broad category tags. Tags are a minor but confirmed ranking signal.
8. Transcript & Captions Upload a full transcript and captions. 94% of top-ranking videos have both. Transcripts are now parsed by AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) for citation.
9. Cards & End Screens Add 1-2 cards linking to related videos at key moments mid-video. Add an end screen linking to the next video in the series (reinforces session extension).
10. Playlist / Series Add the video to the relevant series or playlist for session extension.
11. Pinned Comment Immediately after upload, pin a comment with a question or CTA to seed engagement and boost the comment signal. Example: "Which trend are you most excited to try? Let me know below!"

IV. First 48-Hour Promotion Window

NEW SECTION: YouTube's algorithm weighs early performance heavily. The first 48 hours after upload are critical for signalling to the algorithm that the video deserves wider distribution.
Step Detail & Goal Timing
1. Social Share Share across all social channels (Instagram Stories, TikTok, Facebook) with a direct link to the video. Within 1 hour of upload
2. Email List Send to email subscribers via Klaviyo. Even a small segment drives early views and watch time. Within 2 hours of upload
3. Embed on Site Embed the video on a relevant blog post or collection page on elevenloves.co.uk. This also passes SEO value to both the page and the video. Within 24 hours
4. Community Post Create a YouTube Community post linking to the new video to notify subscribers who may have missed it. 24 hours after upload
5. Reply to Comments Reply to every comment in the first 48 hours. This drives additional engagement signals and encourages more comments. Ongoing for 48 hours
6. Publish Shorts Publish the 2-3 planned Shorts clips with links back to the full video in the description. 24-48 hours after upload

V. AI & LLM Optimisation (2026)

NEW SECTION: AI tools like Google's Gemini, ChatGPT, and Perplexity now parse video transcripts and captions to understand content. They don't just read titles and tags anymore — they listen to what's actually said. This is a new optimisation layer for 2026.
Step Detail & Goal
1. Transcript Clarity Ensure spoken content clearly states topics and directly answers questions. AI tools extract and cite clear, specific language from transcripts.
2. Structured Spoken Content Frame key points as clear statements: "The biggest wearable trend for 2026 is..." or "The best way to style a tulle skirt for a wedding is...". This makes the video citable by AI search engines.
3. Answer Common Questions Incorporate natural answers to "People Also Ask" questions related to your topic. AI tools surface videos that directly answer user queries.
4. Clean Captions Review auto-generated captions for accuracy. AI tools rely on caption text — errors mean your content may be misunderstood or ignored.
Already Working: Eleven Loves' "How to Style a Tulle Skirt" guide is already the #1 AI-cited page on the site (20 ChatGPT pageviews). Applying these principles to video will compound this advantage.

VI. Measurement & Iteration

NEW SECTION: Every video should be reviewed against these metrics at set intervals. Use the diagnosis framework below to identify what to fix.

Performance Review Schedule

Metric Target When to Check If Below Target
Click-Through Rate (CTR) 4-5%+ 48 hours post-upload Swap thumbnail immediately. Revise title if needed.
Audience Retention (30s) 70%+ still watching at 30s 1 week Hook or second hook needs reworking. Review first 30 seconds.
Average View Duration 50%+ of video length 1 week Content pacing issue. Add more pattern breaks, tighten script.
Impressions Trending upward 2 weeks SEO/keyword problem. Check title, description, tags alignment with search demand.
Session Starts Viewers watching more videos after yours Monthly Improve end screen, session extension CTA, and playlist strategy.
Comments Growing per video 1 week Strengthen CTA question. Ensure pinned comment is engaging.

Quick Diagnosis Framework

Low Impressions?
It's an SEO problem. Keywords, title, description, or tags aren't matching search demand.
Low CTR?
It's a thumbnail/title problem. Change the thumbnail first — this alone can lift CTR 2-5% within 48 hours.
Low Retention?
It's a content/pacing problem. Review the retention graph for drop-off points and add pattern breaks.
